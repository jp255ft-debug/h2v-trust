# Namibia Green Hydrogen Reference

## Overview

Namibia is positioning itself as a global leader in green hydrogen production, leveraging its abundant solar and wind resources. H2V-Trust provides verification and certification infrastructure for Namibia's green hydrogen projects.

## Strategic Context

### Why Namibia?

- **Solar Irradiance**: Among the highest in the world (>2,500 kWh/m²/year)
- **Wind Resources**: Consistent coastal winds for wind energy
- **Land Availability**: Vast, sparsely populated areas for renewable installations
- **Strategic Location**: Access to European and Asian markets
- **Water Access**: Coastal desalination capabilities

### Key Projects

1. **Hyphen Hydrogen Energy Project**
   - Location: Tsau //Khaeb National Park
   - Capacity: 3 GW renewable energy
   - Production: 300,000 tons green hydrogen per year
   - Timeline: 2026 initial production

2. **Daures Green Hydrogen Village**
   - Location: Daures Constituency, Erongo Region
   - Focus: Integrated hydrogen and ammonia production
   - Community: Local workforce development

3. **Cleanergy Solutions**
   - Location: Walvis Bay
   - Focus: Green hydrogen for industrial applications
   - Partnership: CMB.TECH

## CBAM Compliance for Namibia

### Requirements

1. **GHG Emissions**: Must demonstrate ≤ 3.4 kgCO₂e/kgH₂
2. **Water Source**: Desalination preferred for coastal projects
3. **Energy Source**: 100% renewable (solar/wind)
4. **Additionality**: New renewable capacity addition

## Regulatory Framework

### Namibian Regulations

- **Green Hydrogen Strategy**: National policy framework
- **Environmental Impact Assessment**: Required for all projects
- **Water Rights**: Regulated by Ministry of Agriculture
- **Land Leases**: Governed by Ministry of Land Reform

### EU CBAM Requirements

- **Reporting**: Quarterly CBAM declarations
- **Verification**: Third-party certification required
- **Penalties**: €50/ton CO₂e for non-compliance
- **Transition Period**: 2023-2025 (reporting only)
- **Full Implementation**: 2026 (financial adjustments)

## Best Practices for Namibian Producers

1. **Early Certification**: Start compliance process early
2. **Continuous Monitoring**: Real-time telemetry data collection
3. **Documentation**: Maintain complete production records
4. **Local Partnerships**: Work with certified verifiers
5. **Community Engagement**: Ensure local benefit sharing
6. **Environmental Stewardship**: Minimize ecological impact
